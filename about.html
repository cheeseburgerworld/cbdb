About CBDB

The idea

Every other review site is a feed built to sell you ads, not a tool built to help you.

No ads. No algorithms. Just burgers.

Here, we flip that. You own your words. Nobody buys their way up. We're not participating in the online advertising economy.

This is an official record of the world's burgers, written by people who actually ate them. Find what's worth your time near you — and places worth seeking out when you're somewhere new.

Different by design
Other platforms own your reviews. They can delete them.
You own yours. Only you can delete them.
Restaurants pay to bury the bad and boost fake or sponsored posts.
No paid placement. No suppression. Every rating public.
Anyone posts, ratings inflate, and five stars stop meaning anything.
Calibrated by real people. Legendary means legendary.
They force you to install an app just to read a review or see a photo.
Read everything in your browser. No app, no login.
Trackers follow you across the web. What you read and tap gets sold to advertisers.
No trackers, no collection, no ad business. Nothing to sell, because nothing's gathered.
A feed tuned to keep you scrolling, not to help you decide.
A database built to answer one question: where's a great burger?
Built in the Atmosphere

Your reviews aren't locked in our database and rented back to you. Each one is posted to your own account in the Atmosphere; what we keep is an index that points back at it. You can take it down at the source anytime — we never owned it.

We don't profile or track you. No ad tech, no data to sell — not as a promise, but because the architecture gives us nowhere to put it. Don't trust our privacy policy. Read our code.

How it works

Sign in, fill out a short form, add a photo of the burger you ate. A review has two parts: the build — what's on it, how it's made — and your take on the value and the visit. It shows up here, on the map and in the database.

The scale has four levels and every one counts. It works like the Michelin guide: the standard never bends to commercial pressure, so the rating means something. ⚡ Legendary is rare by design — overuse kills it. A ㄨ Skip It is just as helpful. A list that only surfaces praise isn't a guide. It's a marketing channel.

Under the hood, every review is a record on the AT Protocol — the network behind Bluesky and the wider Atmosphere — signed by your identity and posted to your own account. We keep an index that points back at it; the record itself is yours. The code is open on GitHub, so anyone can verify exactly what we do with it. Index it, nothing more.
